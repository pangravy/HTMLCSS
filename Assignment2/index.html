Blackness and Apocalypse

PLAYLIST BY JOSHUA BENETT

Black Apocalypse is a classic short fiction that reveals some attitude of the black in local habitations with a focal point on Nigerians. It captures the positive practices adopted by most Africans, and describes possible loopholes which serves as a nexus to connect opportunistic mishaps to these good practices. It describes some inimical attitudes showcased by many acclaimed responsible African men, as well as the effects of neglect by African women on their children; a vice mostly due to an unbearable workload of playing both the fatherly and motherly roles in families. It pictures the most probable attitude of typical Africans living in Africa towards the whites. It exposes the effects of abuse (sexual, religious and cultural) on future relationships, as commonly found in Nigeria. The last chapter laments the effects of segregation between blacks in general. What has been the effect of black to black animosity around you? Does your happiness, just like the fictional Oluebube lie in foreign lands? The writer, Emmanuel Idara Christopher Essien writes with comprehensible words, each with an underlying message which hopes to extol a virtue or fight a vice.
